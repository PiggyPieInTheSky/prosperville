Better Money Habits learning tip #2: Today’s financing and borrowing decisions can have a big impact on long term financial future

What is the long-term difference between buying a $25,000 car vs. your $50,000 (dream) car. Understanding the difference between “wants” and “needs” can make a difference!

Let's say you land that first nice job and you want to reward yourself with a new car purchase. Now let's take a look at how that decision could impact your short- and long-term financial health.

Assumptions:

  1. Financing a $50,000 car vs. $25,000 car and both have:
    1. 5% interest/APR
    2. 20% down payment
    3. 5-year term loan
